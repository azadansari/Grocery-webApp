Broccoli is a unique product. There is more protein in it than in beef, and its quality is in no way inferior to a protein of animal origin. At the same time, the value of broccoli protein increases due to the content of substances that prevent the accumulation of cholesterol in the human body.

   
The eatable part of broccoli, just like in its genetic predecessor – cauliflower, are inflorescences, not leaves. Broccoli surpasses spinach, sweetcorn and asparagus in protein content. The vegetable is rich in carotene, vitamin C and K. The iron content, in comparison with cauliflower and white cabbage, is almost 2 times higher. Broccoli contains antioxidants.
